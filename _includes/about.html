About


I am a senior studying computational chemistry at Carnegie Mellon and an undergraduate researcher in Jeff Gray's Lab at Johns Hopkins. My research fouces on developing computational tools for modeling membrane proteins in Rosetta: specifically, understanding the thermodynamics and chemistry underlying structure and function. Aside from my research, I am passionate about increasing diversity in computational fields, especially by women and students with disabilities. Toward this goal, I actively mentor high school students, co-led a team of Rosetta students to the Grace Hopper Conference for women in computing, and co-developed a program a program to introduce middle school girls to computational thinking.

This spring, I will graduate from Carnegie Mellon this spring and begin my Ph.D. in the fall in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering at Johns hopkins. I hope that my research will contribute greatly to engineering computational tools to investigate diseases and develop drugs to treat them.
